Express Mail

 

Express Mail (EMS) is the quick and efficient way to send urgent business documents and general items deemed 'top priority'.


Express Mail delivers locally and internationally to more than 100 countries around the world and (unlike private courier companies) accepts both documents and parcels.

 

Size & Weight Limitations:

  • Size and weight limits vary from country to country but in general, the sum of the length and the greatest circumference must not exceed 3 metres.
  • In most countries a maximum weight limit of 20 kgs (per item) applies: however, some countries will only accept items weighing 15 kg or less and a few countries impose a maximum limit as low as 10 kg.

 

Services Features:

  • All items accepted by Express Mail are insured (free of charge) against delay, loss and damage.
  • Free Eshaar and mail inquiry service.
  • Track and trace service.
  • Proof of delivery.

 

Service Fees:

  • Rates vary depending on the weight and destination of the postal item, to review the rates chart click here.
  • The same charges apply within any given emirate and between emirates.
  • Post-dated payment facilities and monthly discounts are granted to contracted bodies,  the discount offered ranges from 15% to 40% depending on the volume of mail sent each month.

 

Service Conditions:

The insurance scheme does not cover items that have been lost, damaged or delayed due to forces beyond human control (force majeure) or due to the fault or negligence of the sender.


How to apply:

  • Fill out the application form.
  • Upon successful processing of the new account, the owner will be assigned a special Express Mail account number and will receive several Express Mail labels.
  • Whenever an account holder wishes to send a document or parcel, he/she simply affixes a Express Mail label to the item (writing their Express Mail account number clearly in the space provided) and contact the Express Mail section of Emirates Post to arrange pick-up.
  • Account holders will be billed once every month; bills must be settled within two weeks of the date mentioned on the monthly statement.
