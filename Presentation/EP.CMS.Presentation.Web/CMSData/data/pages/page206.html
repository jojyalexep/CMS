UAE Vice President, Prime Minister and Ruler of Dubai His Highness Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum has honoured a number of public and private sector’s firms and institutions which supported "Absher" initiative launched by UAE President His Highness Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan, aiming to enhance Emirati citizens' involvement in the local labour market.

The honouring ceremony, which saw the presence of Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Presidential Affairs Sheikh Mansour bin Zayed Al Nahyan, was held at Qasr Al Emarat Hoel in Abu Dhabi.

Sheikh Mohammed praised Sheikh Khalifa's initiative, saying that it benefits the citizens and the country. He pointed out that the initiative aims to provide dignified standards of living to UAE citizens, thanking Sheikh Mansour bin Zayed for his support of this great initiative.

Thanking the private sector firms which gave their support to the President's initiative and  contributed to developing and consolidating the national economy, Sheikh Mohammed  called on other private sector’s bodies to give their support for the initiative.

In his address, Sheikh Mansour bin Zayed Al Nahyan said the launch of "Absher" by Sheikh Khalifa reflects his belief that the human being is the basis of development and the cornerstone in all developmental programmes, as he recalled the naming of 2013 as a year of Emiratisation.

Paying tribute to Sheikh Mohammed's participation in the launch of "Absher", Sheikh Mansour said that Sheikh Mohammed is an example of  giving , stressing that the initiative will be complementary to all national efforts springing from the philosophy of empowering and enhancing UAE citizens' competitive capabilities in the labour market. He added that the initiative will create more than 20,000  job opportunities for nationals over the next five years.

Before the end of the ceremony, Sheikh Mohammed and Sheikh Mansour along with youths who benefited from the initiative, posed for photographs. Then Sheikh Mohammed exchanged pleasantries with a number of young male and female beneficiaries and congratulated them for getting jobs, wishing them success.

The launching and honouring ceremony was attended by a number of sheikhs, ministers, and dignitaries and business and community figures in the UAE.
