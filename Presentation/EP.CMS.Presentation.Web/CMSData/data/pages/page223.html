Emirates Post Group, in cooperation with Albarza Centre for the Elderly, which is run by Dubai Health Authority, hosted a function to honor mothers at Albarza Centre on the occasion of International Mother’s Day.

 

The event conveyed greetings to HH Sheikha Fatima Bin Mubarak Al Nahyan, Mother of the Nation, for her unique contribution to nation’s development and women’s empowerment spanning over three decades and managing her responsibilities of grooming national leaders, of whose achievements the country is really proud.

 

On the occasion, various entertainment activities were organized, especially for the mothers, performed by students of Al Saada School.  The poet,  Khalfan Al Washahi, read out a poem which touched the hearts of the audience.

 

A Gulf drama artiste, Saeed Salem, spread the spirit of fun in the hearts of mothers and fathers at the Albarza Centre.  Artist Mohamed Aboud conducted a live painting workshop.

 

Sultan Al Midfa, CEO of Empost and Dr. Salwa Al Suwaidi, Director of the Center bestowed honour of ideal mother on Mrs. Khaltoum Ahmed Faraj, a woman care-giver who has completed 20 years of service at the Centre. Khaltoum is a widow who has brought up seven children on her own, and given them good education.

 

Sultan Al Midfa “This celebration is part of our corporate social responsibility aimed at showing appreciation for the role mothers play in the development of families and community, by instilling values ​​and morality in their children to create a generation that can face all odds and carry out their responsibilities. We would like to thank all the workers at Albarza Centre for providing medical and psychological care to these mothers and fathers.”

 

The recognition of Ideal Mother is an honor for all ideal mothers who deserve more than the title of ideal mother for their role in instilling positive values in their sons and daughters.

 

On her part, Dr. Salwa Al Suwaidi, Director of the Center, praised the initiative of Emirates Post Group in recognizing all mothers in the center and said the program brought a smile on the faces of the people living in the centre.
