In an effort to raise the bar on its services and introduce customer-oriented services, Emirates Post recently hosted a gathering of Strategic Partners and invited them to offer suggestions and ideas for improved services.

Welcoming the partners, Mr. Jeremy Skyrme, Executive Director of Sales, Emirates Post Group, outlined the diversified services of Emirates Post and shared with them glimpses of new projects in the pipeline, as part of Emirates Post’s strategy to tap the growing potential in Postal Mail-related services and International Express.

A questionnaire designed to seek inputs from the partners was distributed on the occasion, and the invitees were asked to provide feedback, to enable Emirates Post to enhance its services.

Among the audience were representatives of Mashreq, Standard Chartered, Dnata, Wall Street Exchange, DHL Express and others.

Mr. Skyrme said Emirates Post was equipped to provide integrated mail-related services, including improved P.O. Box services, mailroom management, printing and mailing of third party e-invoices, direct marketing, bill payments, budget airline bookings, etc.

Referring to the current economic climate, Mr. Skyrme said the UAE was now firmly back on track, and this was reflected in the record number of new registrations for new P.O. Boxes in the first three months of 2013.

Alongside the partner event, Emirates Post convened a meeting of its Retail Team to take stock of performance, and shares ideas on future growth and new developments.
